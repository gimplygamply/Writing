# History (5.5 kya)
## Age of Emergence
For thousands of years, the elves lived off the land of the Predna's North-Western peninsula. These hunter-gatherers primarily lived in minor isolated enclaves, who primarily relied on hunting and gathering from the rich Fradon Jungle while smaller enclaves lived further into the peninsula. Their long-lives and perfectionist nature meant that many of these enclaves remained extremely small all the while mastering artisanal crafts.
Around 5500 ya the Elves of North [[Frada]] began discovering each other and interacting together around the bank of [[Lake Eradia]]. Eventually one enclave would move closer, inciting their previous trade partners to follow and so on until a major trade network of small villages had nested in the region. Few villages could not stray far from their crafts natural resources, resulting in some enclaves separated from the major happenings in the central peninsula but still engaging in long-distance trade.
## Age of Conquest/Expansion
As these highly skilled enclaves formed with their high quality crafts they began to interact. Many engaged in simple trade but before long minor spats began. In time each enclave selected a Den, an elder specialized in their craft would meet with one another and trade partners to discuss resolutions.
The scrupulous deliberation of the elders worked well for politics and trade but the borders and traders required defense, thus formed the Dains. Specialized combatants trained primarily for policing and occasionally warding off savages. Though small in number their intense dedication to combat made them a force to reckon with.
Thus the the Dendainian Hegemony was formed, a distributed network of villages and towns all mingling and trading among themselves with a handful of representatives to protect their interests.

# Culture
## All-Natural Collectivist 
Dendainians suffered heavily from extremely low population numbers due to three main factors:
1. Having become skilled at avoiding and dissuading predators while also having an exceptionally **docile nature**, the Dendainians suffered relatively few non-natural deaths that pressured them into reproducing frequently.
2. The first elves were exceptional gatherers, their natural ties to the land and century old knowledge of their surroundings meant locating food was menial. This typically meant that food stability was never a concern so long as the population grew slower than the food. **This did lead to stigma around having multiple children without it being discussed with the village Den.**
3. Elves were naturally a low fertility species, due to their long life-spans.
The enclaves and their immediate trading partners were often so small that a majority of the Dendains knew each other, the Elves knew they were not in danger from one another and it was common place to welcome an elf never before met to spend intimate moments with an enclave.
The enclaves also took extreme pride in their enclaves crafts, this led to the craft becoming the enclaves identity with members symbolizing their craft in some form. This typically took the form of their specialized tool, customized to their enclaves style. To craft together was expected and to enhance your and others skills was a highly respected and expected act.
The Dendainians hated conflict in all forms, while not pacifists they believed the loss of ability to maintain their skills was to great to even risk sharing a thought that may instigate a minor disagreement. To the Dendainians a hand was seen as the most precious part of the body, pockets were often padded and lined and to pocket ones hands was a standard idle position for the hands. An ultimate threat to a Dendainian was one that involved their hands.
## Close-knit tribes
To be apart of an enclave was to be a part of more than a family, enclaves operated as a single unit. To intentionally harm a kinsmen was a sin worthy of exile if not death and to harm a member of another enclave was to do so to the entire enclave as an entire enclave.
The Dendains were a deeply empathetic people and embraced self-expression and emotional vulnerability. Their close nature meant the repression of emotions could boil over into extreme emotional bursts.
intra-enclave relations were considered very loose and non-binding with the belief being that the enclave should always be as one. This would generate a stigma around inter-enclave relations, physical contact with other enclave members was typically only acceptable if done in a formal and/or platonic manner such as a greeting with no unorthodox romance. Hospitality and camaraderie were still heavily encouraged between enclaves for hegemony and to open doors for orthodox romances in the case of the death of an enclave member.
During the death of a enclave member, two enclaves would agree to a an exchange of kin. The youngest of-age member(s) (Male/Female depending on the requirements of the situation) would be traded off in exchange for either a dowry or an elder craftsman from that enclave in a weeks-long celebration ritual where the enclaves would come together as one for that period. The one exception was in the extremely rare case of a murder, in which case the offending enclave would forfeit their youngest member to maintain peace and once that member bought forth a child would they be given the choice between them or their child returning to the original enclave.
## Artisinal masters
The Dendains worships craft above all forms of busy work. They believed to craft was not only a privilege but a holy-one at that. Other tasks such as construction and foraging were tasks given to junior members, sometimes for an extended duration as a form of punishments. 
### Aurem
To partake in the groups craft was seen as a privilege gifted by [[#Eradiasm|Eradia]] and to be incapable meant to be of a slave-class. Only extensive training as a Dain would mean liberation and even then a enclave already had a Dain the slave would become an Aurem. These slaves would be banished from the hegemony, given the combat skills and a weeks supplies, they were instructed to never return unless they could discover one of Eradia's lost crafts, in which case they would return as the Den of a new enclave. 
A majority  would often die in their first weeks in the wild, Others would join the barbarian tribes of [[Castlia]] while only the most legendary Aurem would return with a craft befitting Eradia.

# Religion
## Eradiasm
- Based on believe that the goddess, Eradia was birthed out of the sky-scraping oak in the middle of the lake.
- She took the saplings of the tree, and used them to build the trees of the word.
	- With the dirt they turned up she built the mountains where she moulded the clouds and blue sky into existence.
	- From the clouds rain she filled the ocean.
	- With the wood of her forests she ignited the sun to create day.
	- She moulded the first animals (to the Dendains, this includes the other races) out of the clay she fired from the sun and earth
	- As her final creation, she mixed the branches from the towering oak until it stood at the height it does today, the clay and the fire of the sun within herself to birth the elves.
- To make sure her people were always safe, she turned her body into the moon Erad and from her blood the red moon Aurel.
	- **Erad** represented her love and care whereas **Aurel** served as the reminder of Eradia's power.
